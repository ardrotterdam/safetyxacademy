What is NEBOSH IGC?

20 January 2025

NEBOSH IGC is one of the most recognised health, safety and environment (HSE) qualifications in the world. But what exactly is NEBOSH IGC? Who is the course for? And why is it so valuable for international HSE professionals and expats who want to work in ports, offshore or on global projects — including those based in the Netherlands and Europe?

NEBOSH stands for the National Examination Board in Occupational Safety and Health. It is a UK awarding body that has been issuing health and safety qualifications since 1979. The NEBOSH International General Certificate (IGC) is recognised in more than 120 countries and is widely regarded as the gold standard for HSE professionals who want to work internationally.

Who is NEBOSH IGC for?

NEBOSH IGC suits a broad group of professionals. You don't necessarily need a prior qualification, though some basic knowledge of occupational health and safety is helpful. Typical candidates include:

  • Expats and international professionals in Europe – Living or working in the Netherlands and want an internationally portable HSE qualification taught in English? NEBOSH IGC travels with you, wherever your career goes next.
  • HSE coordinators in ports and industry – Already working as an HSE officer but missing a globally recognised certificate? NEBOSH IGC significantly raises your market value.
  • People from ports and offshore who want a recognised qualification – Hands-on experience is valuable, but a NEBOSH certificate adds credibility and opens doors to new assignments.
  • Contractors who want to raise their market value – As a self-employed safety professional, NEBOSH IGC makes you more attractive to clients, especially on international projects.
  • Professionals targeting Dubai, Norway, the UK and the Gulf – Many international assignments explicitly require NEBOSH as a minimum certification.

What does the NEBOSH IGC course look like?

The NEBOSH IGC course combines online preparation with classroom sessions. At SafetyXAcademy our international course is taught in English, so you can follow the material wherever you're from. The exam is in English as well — which makes sense, because NEBOSH is an international qualification.

The course is built around several modules that prepare you for the exam:

  • Foundations in Health and Safety
  • HSE Management Systems (ISO 45001 basis)
  • Workplace Hazards
  • Construction & Fire Safety
  • Incident Investigation & Legal Compliance

Expect roughly 100–120 hours of study in total, spread over 3–4 months. Most students combine the course with a full-time job. Our blended approach (online + classroom) keeps it flexible.

Why is NEBOSH valuable for international HSE professionals?

For HSE professionals who want to work in ports, offshore or on international projects, NEBOSH IGC is almost indispensable. Here are the main reasons:

  • Internationally recognised – NEBOSH is accepted in 120+ countries, from Dubai to Norway and from the UK to Singapore. A national qualification mostly works in one country; NEBOSH IGC works worldwide.
  • In demand in ports and offshore – Clients in the ports of Rotterdam and Antwerp increasingly ask for NEBOSH, especially on international projects. Offshore assignments often require it explicitly.
  • Higher day rates as a contractor – Self-employed safety professionals with NEBOSH can often command higher day rates (€600–€1,500 per day) than those without.
  • Career progression – With NEBOSH IGC you can move into senior HSE roles and international projects that would otherwise be out of reach.
  • Taught in English, recognised everywhere – At SafetyXAcademy you get the full course in English, so the material — and your certificate — stays portable across borders.
