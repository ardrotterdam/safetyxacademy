Explained

What is the NEBOSH International General Certificate (IGC)?

NEBOSH is a UK-based examination board that issues HSE qualifications used worldwide. The NEBOSH IGC is its international foundation certificate in occupational health and safety, recognised by employers in 120+ countries.

With NEBOSH IGC you can apply for roles such as HSE officer, safety advisor or safety officer in areas including:

  • Ports & terminals (Rotterdam, Antwerp, Hamburg and beyond)
  • Offshore (oil & gas, offshore wind, support vessels)
  • Industry, chemicals & energy
  • Construction & civil projects

The exam consists of an online open-book theory exam (IG1) and a practical risk assessment (IG2) in which you assess a real workplace. You do not need to travel abroad for the exam — you can sit it from wherever you are based.

Our role at SafetyXAcademy is to prepare you thoroughly: we make the NEBOSH theory clear and practical, practise exam questions together, and coach you through writing your IG2 practical assessment — all in English.

Programme

What you learn during the NEBOSH IGC course

The official NEBOSH syllabus is built around risk management, legislation and practically improving safety on the work floor. We translate that theory into concrete examples from ports, offshore and industry.

1. Foundations & HSE management

Core principles of occupational health and safety, roles and responsibilities, safety policy and HSE culture. You learn how to bring structure to an organisation as a safety professional.

2. Risk assessment & control

Systematically identifying hazards, assessing risk and choosing suitable controls. This also forms the basis for your NEBOSH practical assessment (IG2).

3. Workplace hazards

Physical, chemical, biological and ergonomic risks in sectors such as terminals, factories, construction sites and offshore. With plenty of real-world examples.

4. Process safety, fire & construction

Process safety, fire and explosion risks, hot work, permits to work, working at height and contractor management. Highly relevant for offshore workers and industrial projects.

5. Incidents & continuous improvement

Investigating accidents and near misses, root-cause analysis, reporting and actions to prevent recurrence. This is how you build a safer workplace step by step.

Comparison

NEBOSH IGC versus national certificates and the NEBOSH Diploma

Many visitors want to know how the NEBOSH IGC compares to a local national safety certificate and to the higher NEBOSH Diploma. A short comparison:

Level NEBOSH IGC National certificate NEBOSH Diploma
Type International certificate Country-specific qualification Higher international diploma
Use Entry level HSE / safety officer worldwide Entry level within one country Senior safety / HSE manager roles
SafetyXAcademy focus Yes – this is our specialism No Not yet (possibly later)

In short: if you only ever plan to work in one country, a national certificate can be fine. But if you want the freedom to work across Europe and on international projects (ports, offshore, industry), the NEBOSH IGC is the logical, future-proof choice.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions about the NEBOSH course

What exactly is the NEBOSH IGC course?

The NEBOSH International General Certificate (IGC) is a globally recognised qualification in occupational health and safety. SafetyXAcademy prepares you for it in Rotterdam, fully in English, with an English online open-book exam.

Is the course taught in English?

Yes — lessons, materials and the exam are all in English. You don't need to speak Dutch. The course is designed for international professionals and English-speaking expats.

Is NEBOSH IGC difficult?

It's a serious qualification, but very achievable if you're motivated. Plan for 8–10 hours of self-study per week. During the classroom days in Veerhaven Rotterdam we work through the theory step by step and practise exam questions and the practical assessment.

Can I combine the course with my job?

Yes. The course is designed for working professionals. Part is online at times that suit you, plus a number of classroom days in Rotterdam. Many participants keep working full-time throughout.

Do I need prior qualifications?

No formal prerequisites are required. What helps is an interest in safety and basic knowledge of work processes. Site experience, industry/port background or a technical education all fit well. If in doubt, we run a short intake with you.
